SafeSeats

Project Info:

SafeSeats is an app that helps marginalized communities locate safe restrooms. Users can register for an account, log in, and access a map showing nearby safe restrooms using MapBox and the REFUGE Restroom API. The app displays markers with information about each restroom, including its address, a brief description, and whether it is unisex, ADA accessible, and has a changing table. Users can search for restrooms that meet their specific accessibility needs and add them to their favorites list. They can also view and delete restrooms from their favorites list as needed. The app allows users to get turn-by-turn directions from their current location to the restroom of their choice. SafeSeats aims to make it easier for marginalized communities to find safe, accessible restrooms when they are traveling.

During the project, my primary responsibility was to develop the map and markers feature using MapBox and the REFUGE Restroom API, as well as the search location feature using MapBox's forward geocoding functionality. This involved integrating these tools into the app and configuring them to meet the project's requirements and goals. I also contributed to the testing and debugging of these features to ensure that they were functioning correctly. Overall, my contributions helped to make it easier for users to locate safe restrooms and navigate to them from their current location.
